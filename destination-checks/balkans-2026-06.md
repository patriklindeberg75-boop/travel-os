# Viability Check — Balkans multi-stop (2026-06-16 to 2026-06-29)

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL

CONDITIONAL because Belgrade and Prizren carry a high risk of exceeding the 30°C
single-day activity ceiling during typical late-June conditions. Both stops have
nearby cool escapes (Belgrade: shaded Dorćol + early-morning timing; Prizren:
Sharr Mountains / Prevallë day trips). Heat is managed at the day-plan level, not
a trip-killer.

Budget is realistic but tight: €620–680 estimated against the €700 ground ceiling.

City picks: Kosovo resolved to **Prizren**. North Macedonia left as an open
operator decision — **Ohrid** (cool lake/mountain escape, but ~7–8h multi-leg
bus to Sofia) vs **Skopje** (hot valley city, but ~5h direct bus to Sofia and
its own cool day trips at Matka Canyon / Mt. Vodno). To be locked before
/trip-init scaffolds route_stops.

## Heat flags (per-stop)

- **Belgrade** — max ~28°C but frequently >30°C in late-June heatwaves. FLAG.
- **Prizren** — max ~29°C, city bowl can exceed 30°C; Sharr Mountains cooler. FLAG.
- Budapest (~27°C), Ohrid (~26°C, lake/altitude effect), Sofia (~27°C) — no flag.

## Route note

Overland Serbia → Kosovo is legal and safe on this linear southbound route.
FCDO advises against North Kosovo; Prizren is in the south (normal precautions).
Belgrade safety is US State Dept Level 2 (localized crime/unrest), FCDO normal.
