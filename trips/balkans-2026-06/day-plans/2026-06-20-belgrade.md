## B. Route logic
Zemun is 20–25 min northwest by bus or a cheap taxi — go early while it's cool. Gardoš tower and Grill Loki cluster within a 5-minute walk; do the view first, eat after. From Zemun, head southwest to Ada Ciganlija and Ada Međica island for the swim block — mandatory on a 30°C+ day. Late afternoon drift back east toward Savamala: Splavs on Savski kej sit on the return path, Zaokret is the social anchor, Belgrade Waterfront is a flat evening walk between them. Walking: compact in Zemun, beach-day pace at Ada, flat riverside in Savamala. Transit for the Zemun and Ada legs. Rest pocket: Ada swim block — stay as long as you want, no schedule. Unplanned pocket: the entire afternoon at Ada is structurally open. Weather basis: static dossier forecast (30°C+ flagged for Belgrade in late June).

## C. The plan

**Morning**

- [Gardoš / Millennium Tower — Zemun, Belgrade](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Gardo%C5%A1%20Millennium%20Tower%20Zemun%20Belgrade)
  Old tower for the Zemun-side Danube view, then the swan promenade below · ~45 min · **optional** (but a good reason to be in Zemun early)
  → 5 min walk down to Loki

- [Grill 'Loki' — Zemun, Belgrade](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Grill%20Loki%20Zemun%20Belgrade)
  Legendary street window — big pljeskavica with kajmak and chopped onions, dirt-cheap · quick · **must-do**
  Tip: eat it standing, street-food style. Ask for kajmak on the side for extra. Šopska salata (tomato, cucumber, grated white sirene cheese — the "white cheese thing") is at any sit-down kafana nearby; order it alongside anything.
  → ~30–35 min (bus 84 direction Zemun → Čukarica, or CarGo/Yandex taxi) to Ada Ciganlija

**Midday — swim & shade**

- [Ada Međica — Sava island (Čukarica), Belgrade](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Ada%20Me%C4%91ica%20Belgrade)
  Take a small boat to a car-free forested river island — wild swimming, shade, away from the crowds · half-day · **must-do**
  Tip: the boat crossing is from Ada Ciganlija's western shore; bring small change in dinars. No facilities on the island — bring water and something to eat. If the boat isn't obvious, use the Ada Ciganlija (Makiš) side instead — same area, quieter locals' swim stretch.

- [Ada Ciganlija (Makiš side) — Ada Ciganlija (Čukarica), Belgrade](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Ada%20Ciganlija%20Maki%C5%A1%20Belgrade)
  The cleaner, quieter locals' side of the lake beach — not the crowded strip · backup / combined destination · **must-do**

← **Rest pocket: stay as long as you want. This is the afternoon. Unplanned pocket.**

**Afternoon (when you feel like heading back)**

- [Gavez Club — Savamala, Belgrade](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Gavez%20Club%20Savamala%20Belgrade)
  Bohemian tavern in urban greenery — total shade, dirt-cheap drinks, good coming-back-to-the-city stop · **optional**
  → ~20–25 min transit back toward Savamala/center

**Evening**

- [Splavs on Savski kej — Savski kej (Novi Beograd), Belgrade](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Splavs%20Savski%20kej%20Novi%20Beograd%20Belgrade)
  Ultra-budget sunset beer on a local river barge — breezy, no tourists · **optional**
  → short walk across the bridge toward Savamala

- [Belgrade Waterfront (Beograd na vodi) — Savamala, Belgrade](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Beograd%20na%20vodi%20Savamala%20Belgrade)
  Flat evening stroll through the modern riverside — polished and gentrified but pleasant at dusk · ~30 min · **optional**

- [Zaokret — Savamala, Belgrade](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Zaokret%20Savamala%20Belgrade)
  The meet-people spot — 25–35 backpacker and local crowd, non-touristy, cheap drinks · **optional** (social anchor of the evening)

**Night (if energy allows)**

- [20/44 — Sava riverbank (Brankov most), Belgrade](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=20%2F44%20Sava%20riverbank%20Belgrade)
  Cult alternative floating club — late-night, non-tourist, the real Belgrade river party · **optional** (check tonight first)

## D. Optional add-ons

- [Nikola Tesla Museum — Vračar, Belgrade](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Nikola%20Tesla%20Museum%20Vra%C4%8Dar%20Belgrade)
  Tesla's ashes + live electricity demos, AC, indoor · English tours usually at 10:00 / 12:00 / 14:00 / 16:00 (confirm before going) · **better as a Sunday morning standalone** — Vračar is off today's route and the tour schedule matters.

- **Dedinje / embassy area** [extra — not from your list]: The "fancy embassy neighborhood" you heard about is Dedinje — a leafy hilltop residential district with elegant ambassador villas, quiet tree-lined streets. It's broadly on the way from center toward Ada Ciganlija. The Museum of Yugoslavia (Tito's mausoleum and Tito-era collection) sits in the park there. Worth a brief detour if you're curious; the driver can route past Topčider. Not dossier-vetted but matches what you described.

- **Aviation Museum** → save for Monday: it's right by the airport (Surčin). If your 14:00 bus has you passing there, a morning visit fits perfectly. Cold-War jets in a dome hangar — indoor, AC. Don't detour tomorrow.

- [Silosi — Dorćol (old port / docks), Belgrade](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Silosi%20Dor%C4%87ol%20Belgrade)
  Repurposed Danube grain silos with open-air art · works as a sunset stop if you end up back in Dorćol rather than Savamala.

## Food for tomorrow

**Lunch** → Grill Loki (already the must). Šopska salata at any kafana next door — order it with the pljeskavica or after. Kajmak comes on the meat automatically; ask for extra on the side.

**Afternoon** → bring snacks to Ada (pekara anything, market fruit). The island has nothing.

**Dinner — pick one:**
- [Čedić — central Belgrade](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=%C4%8Cedi%C4%87%20central%20Belgrade) — massive cheap Serbian portions, local institution. Best if you're hungry after the swim. **Recommended if you're in Savamala/center area.**
- [Splav Koliba — Sava riverbank (splav), Belgrade](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Splav%20Koliba%20Sava%20Belgrade) — traditional food on a food barge with a breeze. Best if you want to stay on the river all evening.
- [Radecki — central Belgrade](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Radecki%20central%20Belgrade) — fishermen's club, cheap river fish and beer, zero frills. Pick this if you want a break from grilled meat.
