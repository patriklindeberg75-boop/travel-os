# Sofia — Tuesday, 23 Jun 2026 — half day (afternoon → evening)

**Base:** Hostel Mostel (Makedonia Blvd) — central, walkable to everything below.
**The shape:** Work block is this morning, before you start. From ~1pm: knock out the
Museum of Socialist Art first (it's far + closes ~17:30 + tomorrow is its only window
all trip), then walk an easy, compact central loop — ruins → theatre park → Old Palace
→ Alexander Nevsky — that has no closing pressure and looks best in late-afternoon light.
Dinner in a residential grill, optional drink after.

**Weather basis:** static dossier forecast (Sofia ~27°C, clear). Below the heat ceiling,
so no hard heat-timing — but you sunburn easily, so hat + water for the open sculpture
park, and the central walk is in cooler/shadier late-afternoon hours by design.

**Open pocket:** the City Garden / theatre-park stretch is deliberately loose — sit, watch
the chess players and local life, let the afternoon breathe. Don't rush it.

**Afternoon**

- [Museum of Socialist Art — Izgrev, Sofia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Museum%20of%20Socialist%20Art%20Izgrev%20Sofia)
  Outdoor park of communist-era monumental sculpture + a film archive — unique to Sofia,
  zero-tourist, and genuinely strange in a good way. ~€3–5 · **must-do**
  → Metro back to the centre / Serdika, ~15 min
  Tip: closes ~17:30 and is shut Mondays — Tuesday is your only shot this trip, so do it
  first. Outdoor + exposed: hat, water, sunglasses. Verify hours on the day.

- [Ancient Serdica Complex — Serdika, Sofia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Ancient%20Serdica%20Complex%20Serdika%20Sofia)
  Roman street level under the modern city — open-air ruins at the Largo, free, a 15–20 min
  walk-through. Free · **optional** (your "maybe")
  → ~5 min walk to the City Garden
  Tip: the pedestrian level is always open; the indoor section's hours aren't posted — just
  enjoy the open-air part if it's shut.

- [Ivan Vazov National Theatre + City Garden — Centre, Sofia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Ivan%20Vazov%20National%20Theatre%20Sofia)
  The grand theatre facade fronting Sofia's oldest public garden — fountains, benches, chess
  players, locals out after work. This is your rest pocket. Free · **must-do** · [extra — not
  from your list: central local-life anchor, on the route]
  → ~3 min walk across the garden to the Old Palace
  Tip: grab a coffee from a kiosk and just sit a while — this is the "let the day breathe" stop.

- [Old Palace / National Art Gallery — Battenberg Square, Sofia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=National%20Art%20Gallery%20Sofia)
  The former royal palace on Battenberg Square, now the National Gallery. Worth a look from
  the square regardless; go inside only if you've got time + energy. ~€5–8 · **optional** ·
  [extra — not from your list: on the route, fills the culture gap]
  → ~6 min walk to Alexander Nevsky
  Tip: interior closes ~18:00 (verify) — it's the squeeze stop, so if you're running late just
  enjoy the square and save the gallery for a future Sofia day.

- [Alexander Nevsky Cathedral — Centre, Sofia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Alexander%20Nevsky%20Cathedral%20Sofia)
  Sofia's signature gold-domed Orthodox cathedral — genuinely impressive, free to enter, and a
  living church (not a crowd-ruined trap). Best in late-afternoon/golden-hour light. Free
  (small fee for the crypt icon museum) · **must-do** · [extra — not from your list: holds up
  on its own merits, see note]
  → ~15 min walk NE to Zaimov Park for dinner, or 2 metro/tram stops
  Tip: cover shoulders inside; photography is restricted within the nave. The dome exterior at
  ~6–7pm is the shot.

**Evening**

- [SkaraBar 1 — Zaimov Park, Sofia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=SkaraBar%201%20Zaimov%20Park%20Sofia)
  Residential-neighbourhood grill in Zaimov Park — mixed grill + mezze, away from every tourist
  cluster. Your proper Bulgarian dinner. ~€8–11 · **must-do**
  → KANAAL is ~15 min back toward the centre; Hostel Mostel ~20 min / short metro
  Tip: order the **solo mixed grill** (not the table set) to stay on budget, and arrive by
  19:00 or be ready to wait for outdoor seating.

- [KANAAL — Centre, Sofia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=KANAAL%20Centre%20Sofia)
  Outdoor bar in a repurposed canal — backpacker + local young crowd, your one social-evening
  option. ~€3–5/drink · **optional**
  Tip: ask Mostel reception for the current location before you go. First night out, so no
  party-cap worry — but you've got a hike-heavy weekend coming, so keep it easy.

### Food, beyond the stops above

Dishes to actually order while you're here:
- **Meshana skara** — the mixed grill, your dinner at SkaraBar 1.
- **Banitsa** — flaky cheese pastry; grab one hot from a bakery on a morning (not a cafe).
- **Tarator** — cold yogurt-cucumber soup; perfect after the exposed sculpture park. Order at
  any traditional restaurant.
- **Shkembe chorba** — tripe soup, a Sofia institution if you're feeling adventurous (morning).
- **Lyutenitsa** — roasted-pepper-and-tomato relish that shows up on every table; eat with bread.
