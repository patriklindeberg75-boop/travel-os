# Sofia — Wednesday, 24 Jun 2026 — food-led last day → evening train to Plovdiv

A food pilgrimage through your last Sofia day: banitsa + boza breakfast, two museums
(communist sculpture park + national history), the Women's Market for lunch, and the
tripe-soup institution — then the ~19:00–20:00 train to Plovdiv. Indoor museums sit in
the warm midday hours; food is the thread that runs through it all.

**Morning**

- [Banicharnitsa — Oborishte, Sofia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Banicharnitsa%20Oborishte%20Sofia)
  Highest-rated banitsa in Oborishte — your flaky-cheese-pastry breakfast · ~€2 · **must-do**
  Get a **boza** (fermented millet drink) alongside it — the classic Bulgarian breakfast pairing.
  → ~5–10 min walk to a work café in Oborishte
  Tip: Mon–Fri only, opens 06:00 — go early while it's hot and fresh.

- **Work block (protected, ~1–1.5h)** — any Oborishte café, the best WiFi of the trip
  Front-loaded so the rest of the day is free. This is the flex item: drop it if you'd
  rather work from Plovdiv on Thursday and let today breathe.
  → Metro NE toward Izgrev, ~15–20 min

- [Museum of Socialist Art — Izgrev, Sofia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Museum%20of%20Socialist%20Art%20Izgrev%20Sofia)
  Outdoor sculpture park of communist-era monumental art + film archive — unique to Sofia · ~€3–5 · 2h · **must-do (hours unverified)**
  ⚠ **Confirm Wednesday hours before making the Izgrev trip.** The dossier only vetted a
  Tuesday window for this trip and flagged it as the sole confirmed slot; Sofia museums
  usually close Mondays only (so Wednesday is *likely* fine), but this isn't verified —
  call or check online before you ride out to Izgrev. **If it's closed Wednesday:** skip
  the Izgrev leg and start the day at the Women's Market instead — the rest of the route
  is unaffected.
  → Metro back toward the centre, ~20 min, to the Women's Market
  Tip: the sculptures are open-air, so this is your one exposed stop — fine at 27°C, hat for the UV.

**Midday**

- [Women's Market (Zhenski Pazar) — Zhenski Pazar, Sofia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Women%27s%20Market%20Zhenski%20Pazar%20Sofia)
  The daily locals' market — assemble a Bourdain-style lunch from the stalls · ~€5 · 1–1.5h · **must-do**
  Buy **lyutenitsa + a wedge of sirene + Bulgarian yogurt** to take on the train.
  → ~10 min walk; this long lunch is your rest pocket
  Tip: hot-food stalls (grilled corn, burek) are near the central aisle; produce on the
  perimeter. Keep phone + wallet in front pockets in the crowd.

- [Shkembedzhiinitsa — Sveta Troitsa, Sofia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Shkembedzhiinitsa%20Sveta%20Troitsa%20Sofia)
  Tripe-soup (shkembe chorba) institution — a stripped-down Sofia food ritual · ~€3 · quick · **must-do**
  → ~15 min by metro/taxi back to the central cluster
  Tip: **cash only — carry small euro/lev notes.** Closes 16:00, Mon–Fri. This is a
  westward detour from the centre — it's the one stop stretching the day's footprint, so
  it's the natural trim if the day feels heavy. A small bowl is enough; you've eaten well already.
