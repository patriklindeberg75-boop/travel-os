### More Bulgarian food to try (Plovdiv & the Thracian plain)

Plovdiv sits in Bulgaria's wine-and-veg heartland, at the foot of the Rhodope
mountains — so beyond the staples, there's location-bound stuff you can't get as
well elsewhere. **★ = local to Plovdiv/Thrace/Rhodopes — eat it here.**

Staples worth hitting:
- **Banitsa or mekitsi** — flaky cheese pastry / fried dough, the local breakfast.
- **Tarator** — cold cucumber-yogurt soup; the perfect >30°C lunch starter.
- **Shopska salad** — tomato, cucumber, pepper, grated sirene; the national salad.
- **Meshana skara / kebapche + kyufte** — mixed grill; order with **ayran**.
- **Lyutenitsa** — roasted-pepper-and-tomato relish; eat with bread, not as a course.

★ Local to here — prioritise these:
- **★ Mavrud wine** — a dark, spicy red from a grape grown almost only around
  Plovdiv/Thrace. Order a glass with dinner — this is the location-bound one.
- **★ Kamenitza beer** — Plovdiv's own brewery (since 1881); the hometown lager.
- **★ Patatnik** — grated-potato cake baked with cheese and herbs, a Rhodope classic.
- **★ Smilyanski bob (Smilyan beans)** — fat, sweet beans from a Rhodope village,
  cooked as a rich bean stew (bob chorba); a regional point of pride.
- **★ Sirene po shopski** — sirene cheese baked in a clay pot with tomato, pepper
  and a cracked egg on top; bubbling, simple, everywhere here.
- **★ Katino meze** — pork and mushrooms in a clay pot, a Plovdiv-region tavern dish.
- **Rakia** — grape brandy, the table spirit; sipped slowly before the meal, not after.
- **Garash cake or snezhanka** for dessert; **boza** if you want the old-school
  fermented breakfast drink (try it at the Turkish café by Dzhumaya Mosque).
