All Bulgarian dishes and drinks surfaced in the Pass 4a triage. Numbered for reference.

1. Banitsa — filo pastry with cheese (sirene) filling; the quintessential Bulgarian breakfast pastry
2. Ayran — cold salted yogurt drink; canonical banitsa pairing
3. Boza — fermented wheat drink; thick, malty, slightly sour; traditional pairing with banitsa
4. Tarator — cold cucumber-yogurt soup; herb-forward; light and refreshing; summer staple
5. Shopska salata — tomato, cucumber, pepper, onion topped with grated white cheese; ubiquitous fresh salad

7. Kyopolou — roasted aubergine and pepper spread; smoky, earthy; cold mezze
8. Kebapche — grilled minced meat roll (pork or mixed); the Bulgarian skara standard
9. Kyufte — grilled minced meat patty (pork or mixed); skara sibling to kebapche
10. Dyuner — Bulgarian döner kebab / gyro wrap; cheap street-food lunch
11. Shkembe chorba — tripe soup; traditional hangover/morning soup; OFFAL — operator confirmation required before targeting

13. Banski starets — Bansko cured sausage; regional specialty; spiced and dried pork
14. Katino meze — Bansko cold mezze plate: cured meats, cheese, roasted peppers, pickles
15. Chomlek — slow-cooked clay-pot stew (typically pork or lamb with vegetables); Bansko / Rhodope tradition
16. Melnik wine — from the southwestern wine region; dark red from Shiroka Melnishka Loza grape; notable regional product
17. Mursalski chay (Mursal tea) — high-altitude Pirin herbal tea; brewed from Sideritis (ironwort); endemic to Pirin and western Rhodopes; order at any mehana in the Bansko area
18. Rilska pastarva — mountain trout from Rila rivers; freshwater fish; the defining Rila food signature; grilled or baked whole

21. Melo / Rhombus craft beer — small Bulgarian craft producers; better alternative to Pirinsko if available

## TARGET SET — Dishes to Drive the Venue Search (by Stop)

### SOFIA (Stop 1 — 2 nights, Mon 22–Tue 23)

Target the everyday city staples. Sofia is where to anchor the Bulgarian food baseline before heading into the mountains.

**Breakfast anchor:**
- Banitsa + ayran (or boza) — the non-negotiable Bulgarian morning. Find a dedicated banitsa bakery, not a cafe. Women's Market area has stalls. Freshest in the morning; the dough should be flaky-hot, not soggy.

**Cold mezze / light lunch:**
- Tarator — a bowl of this cold soup in summer is one of the better things you will eat. Any decent traditional restaurant (mehana) or even a sit-down cafe does it. Order it as a starter or a standalone lunch.
- Snezhanka and kyopolou — cold mezze plates; order as appetisers at any mehana stop. These are the healthy-ish end of the Bulgarian starters list.
- Shopska salata — the default fresh salad; fine at almost any place, but better at venues using seasonal local tomatoes.

**Main / street lunch:**
- Skara grill (kebapche + kyufte) — find a proper skara grill, not a tourist-menu place. Look for a mehana or a dedicated grill spot in a local-facing neighbourhood. These are lunch or early-dinner dishes; portion-sized for one.
- Dyuner — cheap street-food lunch fallback; a good Bulgarian dyuner is solid value; Women's Market area and surrounding streets are the right zone.

**FLAG — Shkembe chorba:** Tripe soup. Traditionally a morning-after or early-morning dish at dedicated tripe-soup spots (chorbadjiinitsa). Only target this if the operator actively eats offal. Needs confirmation. If yes, Sofia is the right city for it — it is less common in Bansko and Rila.

### BANSKO / PIRIN (Stop 2 — 3 nights, Wed 24–Fri 26)

Regional signatures are the reason to eat properly here. Avoid resort-facing tourist menus; go to the village-mehana end of the market.

**The must-eat Bansko dishes:**
- Banska kapama — the centrepiece regional dish; slow-braised pork, sauerkraut, and sausage in a sealed clay pot; portions are large and heavy. Target this for a post-hike dinner when hungry. Confirm it is house-made on clay-pot principle, not a tourist-menu approximation. Good to share; a single portion is usually more than enough.
- Banski starets — the Bansko cured sausage; order it on a cold plate or as part of katino meze. A quick, cheap way to taste the regional charcuterie.
- Katino meze — the cold mezze spread (cured meats, cheese, peppers, pickles). Standard in any proper Bansko mehana. Good as a shared starter or a light solo meal before a harder day.
- Chomlek — clay-pot slow-cooked stew; heavier than kapama; interchangeable for the post-hike dinner slot.

**Trail fuel:**
- Banderitsa (Banderishka Polyana) — the trailhead hut cafe en route to Vihren Chalet; basic food and drinks; the stop before the Pirin hike ascent. Not a destination in itself, but carry forward as the trail-fuel anchor.

**Drinks:**
- Mursalski chay (Mursal tea) — order this at the mehana, not a coffee chain. It is endemic to Pirin; the right place to try it.
- Melnik wine — available in most mehanas in Bansko even though Melnik is further south. A glass with kapama or chomlek is the correct pairing.
- Pirinsko beer: mass-market; skip if Melo or Rhombus is on tap. Note: craft beer in Bansko is thin. Do not expect KANAAL-level craft selection here.

**Carry-forward venue candidates (from Pass 3 cross-flag):**
- Zehtindjieva Kashta — traditional mehana; local; budget-friendly; assess in Pass 4b
- Dedo Tase — village-style; cheap; backpacker-frequented; assess in Pass 4b
- Mehana Dedo Yonkata — addendum candidate from Pass 3; assess in Pass 4b

### RILA MOUNTAINS / PANICHISHTE (Stop 3 — 2 nights, Sat 27–Sun 28)

Food options here are thin — mountain hut and small local-restaurant scale. One regional dish stands out as the genuine signature.

**The must-eat Rila dish:**
- Rilska pastarva (mountain trout) — fresh trout from Rila rivers; grilled or baked whole with herbs; the divergent food signature of this stop. Find the closest legitimate restaurant to Panichishte that serves freshly sourced trout (not frozen). Valyavitsa is the carry-forward candidate for this.
- Kiselo mlyako s borovinki — Bulgarian mountain yogurt with wild blueberries; available at huts near the Seven Lakes and at any simple place around Panichishte. Light, energising, a genuine mountain-food moment. Good breakfast or post-hike snack.

**Carry-forward venue candidate (from Pass 3 cross-flag):**
- Valyavitsa — traditional restaurant near Rila; post-hike meal; the trout anchor candidate; assess in Pass 4b

**Note on scope:** Rila is a 2-night stop with limited restaurant infrastructure. One proper sit-down meal (the trout) and a hut snack (yogurt + berries) is realistic. Do not over-engineer the food itinerary for this stop.

## Flags and Notes

**Shkembe chorba (tripe):** Offal dish. Targeted only if operator explicitly eats offal. Needs operator confirmation before including in the Pass 4b venue search.

**Banska kapama — large portion / holiday dish:** Typically cooked in quantity; a full portion is heavy. Best targeted for dinner after a full hike day when appetite is high. Confirm it is made to order on the day, not pre-cooked in batch. At some tourist-trap mehanas it is a menu prop rather than a properly made dish — ask the staff whether it is house-made.

**Pirinsko beer vs. craft:** Pirinsko is the local mass-market lager — fine as a cold beer on a hot day but not a craft-beer moment. In Bansko, look for Melo or Rhombus if the venue has them; in Sofia, KANAAL and Crafter Bar (Pass 3 keeps) are the right destinations for craft beer.

**Anti-resort-pricing note for Bansko food:** The same pricing distortion that affects activities applies to food. Some mehanas facing the main resort square charge resort prices for what are peasant dishes. The target venues should be on side streets or in the old town quarter, not on the main gondola-adjacent strip.
